    Transition Housing at Jacob's House¶

    Your First Step Toward Recovery¶

    Transition Housing at Jacob’s House provides a structured and supportive environment where recovery takes precedence. Designed for individuals in the early stages of sobriety, this program focuses on stabilizing lives, fostering accountability, and laying the groundwork for long-term success.

    What Transition Housing Offers¶

    1. A Focus on Recovery
      Residents are encouraged to prioritize sobriety through:

    2. Daily check-ins and progress monitoring.

    3. Mandatory attendance at recovery meetings.

    4. A drug-free, structured living environment.

    5. Structure and Accountability
      Clear rules create stability:

    6. Adherence to a sober living contract, not a traditional lease.

    7. Participation in chores and community responsibilities.

    8. Curfews and other guidelines to maintain a safe space.

    9. Support Without Immediate Financial Pressure

    10. Initial program fees are often covered by government assistance.

    11. Residents pay a program fee, reinforcing the focus on recovery rather than traditional tenancy.

    12. A Safe Space to Begin Again
      "This isn’t about finding a job right away—it’s about stabilizing your life." – Jason Jacobs

    Residents can focus entirely on recovery without the stress of immediate employment, allowing them to rebuild a solid foundation.

    Expectations for Residents¶

    At Jacob’s House, every resident commits to a set of rules designed to foster recovery and personal growth. These expectations ensure a supportive, structured environment where everyone can thrive.

    What Residents Must Do:¶

    • Sign a Sober Living Contract
    • Residents agree to sobriety, meeting attendance, and adherence to house rules.
    • Attend Recovery Meetings
    • Participation in daily or weekly meetings is mandatory to build focus and accountability.
    • Stay Clean and Respect Rules
    • No drugs, alcohol, weapons, or fighting are permitted.
    • Contribute to the Community
    • House chores and responsibilities are shared among all residents.
    • Focus on Recovery First
    • During the initial months, residents concentrate solely on building healthy habits without the immediate pressure of finding work.

    What Jacob’s House Provides in Return¶

    1. A Drug-Free, Safe Environment
      Residents are surrounded by like-minded individuals in a space designed for recovery.

    2. Community and Support
      Peer relationships and house managers create a family-like atmosphere of mutual accountability.

    3. Structure to Rebuild Lives
      A disciplined approach to recovery helps residents develop responsibility and independence.

    Why Transition Housing Matters¶

    Transition Housing is the foundation of recovery at Jacob’s House.

    "This is the starting point, the foundation. It’s where you get clean, stabilize, and start rebuilding your life in a safe, supportive environment."
    – Jason Jacobs

    By focusing on recovery first, residents develop the stability and skills needed to transition to the next phase of the program.
